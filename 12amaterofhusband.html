A Matter Of Husband

Summary


Author: Ferenc Molnár

Genre: One-act play

Setting: The drawing room of a famous Hungarian actress

"A Matter of Husbands" is a witty one-act play by Hungarian playwright Ferenc Molnár. The narrative unfolds in the drawing room of a renowned actress, where the central characters—a young, earnest woman and the famous actress—engage in a conversation that reveals the complexities of relationships and the art of deception.

The play opens with the earnest young woman anxiously waiting in the actress's drawing room. She is distraught, believing that her husband, Alfred, a lawyer, has been unfaithful to her with the actress. Upon the actress's arrival, the young woman confronts her, demanding that she return her husband.

The actress, maintaining composure, denies any romantic involvement with Alfred. She explains that she knows him professionally, as he is handling a legal matter for her. The young woman insists that Alfred has been sending her flowers and letters, which she believes are signs of an affair.

In response, the actress spins a tale, suggesting that Alfred is merely trying to make his wife jealous to rekindle their relationship. She portrays the situation as a common theatrical ruse, where men use actresses to provoke jealousy in their wives.

Initially skeptical, the earnest young woman begins to believe the actress's story. She apologizes for her earlier accusations and expresses gratitude for the actress's understanding. As she prepares to leave, the actress calls out to Alfred, who emerges from the closet, revealing that he has been hiding there throughout the conversation.

The play concludes with the revelation that the actress and Alfred were indeed involved, and the entire scenario was a staged act to manipulate the young woman. This twist highlights themes of deception, jealousy, and the blurred lines between reality and performance.

Characters

  • Earnest Young Woman: The wife who believes her husband is having an affair.
  • Famous Actress: The woman whom the young wife suspects of being involved with her husband.
  • Alfred: The young woman's husband, a lawyer with ties to the actress.

Themes

  • Jealousy: The play explores how jealousy can cloud judgment and lead to misunderstandings.
  • Deception: It delves into how appearances can be manipulated, and truths can be concealed.
  • Illusion vs. Reality: The narrative blurs the lines between what is real and what is staged, reflecting the theatrical nature of life.

Conclusion

"A Matter of Husbands" is a cleverly crafted play that uses humor and irony to examine the complexities of relationships. Through its unexpected twist, it challenges the audience to consider the nature of truth and the ease with which one can be deceived by appearances.

Reference to the context


a. Sketch the character of Famous Actress.

The Famous Actress is a young, renowned, and beautiful actress who is envied by the ordinary woman. On the outside, she looks innocent and harmless but in reality, she is mean and wicked. She is cunning and deceitful as seen in the way she concocts a story to sway the woman from believing that her husband was having an affair with her. She also lives an immoral life as seen in the conclusion of the story where it was revealed that Alfred, the ordinary woman’s husband was actually in her house.

b. Shed light on the difference between an ordinary woman and an actress.

In the story, “A Matter of Husband”, the ordinary woman is portrayed as a simple-minded and timid person who was easily deceived by the false story told by the actress. She had come to the actress in tears completely sure that her husband was having an affair with the actress. However, the actress told a story to her of how her husband was trying to regain the love he had for her by doing things to get her jealous. Without asking questions, the ordinary woman believed her and bought into the story. In the end, it was revealed that her husband was actually in the actress’s room. This shows that the actress was cunning while the ordinary woman was timid.
